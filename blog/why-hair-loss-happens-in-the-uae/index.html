Scalp & Recovery · Journal

Why hair loss happens in the UAE.

You have noticed more hair than usual, in the brush, on the pillow, in the shower, and it started around the time you settled here. It is an unsettling thing to watch, and the worry can grow quietly. Before it does, here is a calm look at what may be going on, and the gentle first step to understanding it.

What you might have been told

That it is stress, or the water, or just something you have to accept now that you live here. That there is nothing to be done, or that you should buy the first scalp product you can find and hope.

Here’s what’s actually happening

Often, the concern sits at the scalp rather than the strand. The scalp is skin, and like the skin on your face it has an environment that can tip out of balance. When it does, the hair growing from it can struggle. So a scalp that feels tight, flaky or oily, or a season of more shedding than usual, is frequently a sign that the environment at the root has shifted, not that anything is broken. Reset that environment and the hair often responds.

A new climate can play its part. Hard water and a big change in surroundings are real, and they can leave the scalp less settled than it was at home. That is the environment at work, not a failing in you.

One honest note. Shedding and thinning can have many causes, and some sit with your health, not your hair, things like changes after having a baby, thyroid, iron levels, medication or stress. Those are for a doctor, and if your shedding is sudden, patchy or heavy, seeing your GP first is always the right move. What a salon can do is read the scalp and the hair, care for the environment where that is the story, and tell you honestly when something is better looked at by a doctor.

It’s no wonder you didn’t know

Scalp care is rarely explained. Most women are sold shampoo, not an understanding of the skin their hair grows from, and nobody separates the part a salon can help with from the part that belongs with a doctor. So the worry sits with you, unnamed. It is no wonder it felt confusing. That was never yours to untangle alone.

What this means for you

The first step is not a product, it is understanding. At a consultation your stylist reads your scalp and your hair, names what they can see, and where the concern is the scalp environment, prescribes the reset that supports it, the way a facialist would calm and rebalance skin that had tipped out of sorts. And if what they see is better checked by a doctor, they will tell you plainly and kindly. You stop guessing, and you get a clear, honest picture of your own hair.

The Tara Rose Truth

Your scalp is skin. Read it, care for the environment, and the hair responds.

The questions we hear most
Why am I shedding more since moving to the UAE?

A new climate and hard water can leave the scalp less settled than it was at home, and an unsettled scalp environment can show as more shedding. It is often the environment, not your hair failing.

Is this something a salon can fix?

Where the concern is the scalp environment, a salon can read it and care for it. Where the cause may sit with your health, we will tell you honestly and point you to a doctor first.

When should I see a doctor instead?

If your shedding is sudden, patchy, heavy, or comes with other changes in how you feel, please see your GP first. Some causes are medical, and those come before any salon care.
